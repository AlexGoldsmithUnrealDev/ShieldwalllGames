Shieldwall Games Ltd – Our Story
Shieldwall Games was not born in a boardroom. There were no investors, no publishers, no venture capital. There was no office, no staff, no formal training. Just four friends — tired of waiting for someone else to make the kind of game we wanted to play — and one burning idea in the mind of a storyteller named Alex.

At the beginning, we were outsiders. None of us had a game dev degree. None of us worked at a major studio. We didn’t have pipelines or production schedules — just notebooks full of lore, nights filled with dreams, and a stubborn belief that we could teach ourselves how to build the worlds we saw in our heads. Some people call it naive. We call it necessary.
The story of Shieldwall Games is a story of rebellion — against an industry that too often puts profit above passion, that demands crunch instead of creativity, and where real gamers are too often locked out of decision-making. We knew we couldn’t change that system from within. So we built something outside it.
We started with Ashborn: Fire & Blood, our first game and the opening chapter of a much larger saga — one that spans centuries, continents, and mythologies across eight planned titles. We aren’t just building games; we’re crafting an epic. Each entry is part of a connected world, carefully plotted from the start, with choices and events that echo across generations. Our players won’t just experience a story — they’ll become part of a legacy.
But where we are today? We’re still small. Still indie. Still unfunded. Everything we’ve made so far has been built from scratch, powered by learning, iteration, and sheer stubborn determination. Our prototype is self-made in Unreal Engine 5.6 — not because someone paid us to do it, but because it had to be real. It had to exist. We are still learning. Every day. Every system. Every animation and AI behavior is a brick in the foundation of something far greater.
We work late. We work weird hours. But we work joyfully. Because this isn’t just a project — it’s the culmination of years of imagination, forged into code by people who care more about narrative, history, and heart than shareholders. We’ve turned our garages and bedrooms into development caves. We've laughed through bugs, cried through crashes, and built something real with no safety net.

Our Dream
Shieldwall Games isn’t just a studio. It’s a philosophy — that games should be made by people who live and breathe them. That developers should be treated with respect and dignity. That studios can thrive without selling their soul to the highest bidder.
We’re building toward a future where we can operate from a self-sustaining, eco-conscious studio. Solar-powered. Carbon-offsetting. A creative space filled with plants, sunlight, and peace — where Employee Morale Officers like dogs and cats roam freely, not just because we love animals, but because we believe work should feel human.
We want to make the kind of company where nobody dreads Mondays. Where flexible hours aren’t a perk — they’re the baseline. Where if you’re passionate, kind, and a true gamer at heart, you belong. We don’t believe in expanding fast or hiring en masse. Every person who joins Shieldwall is handpicked — not just for skill, but for soul. We want our team to feel like family. And families don’t burn out. They grow.
One day, we hope to be able to pay people generously, give them flexibility to live their lives, and provide creative freedom to tell the stories burning inside them. Our office won’t just be a workplace. It will be a home for storytellers, game designers, artists, writers, and tinkerers who believe in something better.

Our Values
No Publishers. Ever.
No marketing executive will ever override our story. No investor will tell us to cut a character because she doesn’t “test well.” We believe that only gamers should guide game development — and we are gamers, first and always.

Nature Comes First.
As we grow, we are committed to running our studio sustainably. We want to lead by example — proving that even digital worlds can be built without damaging the real one. Our goal is a zero-footprint HQ that gives back more than it takes.

History Matters.
Though our worlds are fictional, our inspirations are not. We draw from real cultures, ancient traditions, and forgotten peoples. We commit to honoring those histories, not appropriating them. Our fantasy is built on respect and research.

Work Should Feel Worthwhile.
We will never crunch our team. We will never put deadlines above dignity. We don’t believe in sacrificing health for hype. Our success won’t be measured in quarterly reports — it’ll be measured in joy, community, and creative fulfillment.

We are working in stages. First, completing our vertical slice for Ashborn: Fire & Blood — a playable prototype that proves our systems, vision, and artistic direction. This will fuel our community outreach, early funding efforts, and — when we’re ready — a possible Kickstarter campaign. Not because we need validation. But because we want to invite people into the saga.
From there, we’ll launch Ashes as our flagship title. If it succeeds, we reinvest — not into buildings or bloat, but into tools, time, and talent. We grow slowly. Intentionally. Sustainably.
With each game, we tell more of our world’s story. And through it all, we stay independent. We never chase trends. We never compromise our world for monetization. No loot boxes. No NFTs. No battle passes that drain rather than delight. Just immersive, brutal, story-rich worlds that gamers can truly lose themselves in.
