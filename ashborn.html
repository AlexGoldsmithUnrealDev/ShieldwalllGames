The Game

Where History Meets the Tabletop

Ashborn: Halls of Saga is a tabletop wargame set in the heart of the Viking Age. It is not a game about mythology or fantasy. It is a game about iron, oath, and consequence — about the men and women who stood in shield walls, raided coastlines, and carved kingdoms from a world that gave nothing freely.

The design philosophy behind Ashborn is simple: historical accuracy should never come at the cost of playability, and tactical depth should never require a degree to understand. Every rule in Ashborn exists because the history demanded it. Combat is brutal, decisive, and grounded in the realities of early medieval warfare — the push and shove of the shield wall, the terror of a flank collapsing, the desperate gamble of a champion's challenge.

But Ashborn is more than a battle game. Between engagements, your warband grows. Warriors gain scars, reputations, and grudges. Skalds compose verses about your greatest victories and most shameful defeats. Over time, your warband's saga becomes something uniquely yours — a story told not by the designers, but by the dice, the decisions, and the iron.

Ashborn is the debut title from Shieldwall Games — a studio founded on the belief that history is the greatest game ever played, and it deserves to be played well.

Features

Forged in the Age of Axes

Four pillars that define what Ashborn is — and what it refuses to be.

Tactical Combat

The shield wall is not a formation — it is a living, breaking thing. Ashborn's combat system captures the brutal push and pull of early medieval warfare, where positioning matters more than luck and every gap in the line can mean annihilation.

Saga-Driven Narrative

Your warband is not a list of stats — it is a story. Between battles, warriors earn reputations, skalds compose verses, and your saga grows with every victory and every loss that shapes your company's legend.

Historical Fidelity

Every weapon, every piece of armour, every tactic in Ashborn is drawn from the archaeological and literary record of the Viking Age. We do not invent history — we build from what the evidence tells us was real.

Accessible Depth

Ashborn is designed to welcome new players with clear rules and fast setup, then reward experienced wargamers with layers of tactical and narrative depth that reveal themselves over many campaigns.

The Setting

The Age of Axes

Environment Art Coming Soon

The eighth century is ending. Across Scandinavia, a generation of warriors is looking outward — to the silver-rich monasteries of Northumbria, to the trade routes of the Rus, to the fractured kingdoms of Francia. The age of the Viking has begun, but it is not the age of horned helmets and berserker rage that popular culture remembers. It is an age of calculated ambition, blood-feuds settled across generations, and the iron discipline of the shield wall.

Ashborn does not reimagine the Viking Age — it presents it. The gods are present not as magical forces but as the cultural framework through which warriors understood their world. The raids are not adventures — they are economic necessities driven by limited farmland and harsh winters. This is history as it happened: complex, violent, human, and endlessly compelling. The drama does not need embellishment. It needs only to be told.
