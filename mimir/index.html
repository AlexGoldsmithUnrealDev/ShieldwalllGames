Everything in one place

One world. Every tool. Infinite stories.

Mimir is a desktop application for structured worldbuilding. Your entries live as plain markdown files with YAML frontmatter — readable in any editor, ready for AI, and native to any game engine. 13+ built-in entry types. Custom schemas. Five relationship trees. Interactive maps. A full quest and dialogue graph editor with simulation mode. Available on Windows, macOS, and Linux.

AI Tool Integration (MCP) & Engine Export

From your world
to your engine, instantly.

Mimir's MCP server (Model Context Protocol — an open standard for connecting AI tools to your data) gives Claude, Cursor, and other AI tools direct access to your world data. Export to Unreal Engine DataTable CSV and generic JSON at V1, with Unity ScriptableObject and Godot Resource exports coming in V1.5.

Custom Schemas

Build any world,
your way.

13+ built-in entry types — characters, species, cultures, places, factions, artifacts, events, languages, story arcs, quests, dialogues, maps, and more. Create unlimited custom entry types via the schema editor with 9 field types including text, dates, links, link lists, tags, and relationship lists. No developer skills needed. Quest & Dialogue Editor

Design narratives
that ship to your engine.

A node-based quest and dialogue graph editor built on React Flow. 8 node types including dialogue lines, branching choices, conditions, consequences, and subgraph references. A typed variable system with 4 scopes (global, per-character, per-faction, per-place). Full simulation mode with step debugger, state inspector, coverage tracking, and replay logging. Direct engine export to Unreal, Unity, and Godot.

  • ✦  8 node types with drag-and-drop visual editing
  • ✦  Typed variables — global, per-character, per-faction, per-place
  • ✦  Simulation mode with step debugger and coverage tracking
  • ✦  Subgraph references (Inkle knots/stitches pattern)
  • ✦  Direct export to Unreal, Unity, and Godot

Ask Mimir — AI Assistant

Ask your world
anything.

Ask Mimir is the in-app AI assistant that knows your world. Built on a strict-honesty invariant — it answers only from your lorebible content and confesses ignorance with stub-creation suggestions. Three quality tiers (Fast, Quality, Ultimate). Per-entry focused conversations. Prompt caching for token savings. Pay-as-you-go credits on the free tier, monthly quota on Studio.

Language Translator

Bring your invented
languages to life.

A full constructed-language workbench. Define registers, cultural bias, grammar rules, naming conventions, pronoun hierarchy, and vocabulary per language. Translate English text into your invented language — consistently, every time. AI captures new vocabulary automatically. Per-word regenerate controls, pronunciation popover, 500-entry history. Calibration phrases let you set the exact tone. Pay-as-you-go credits on free tier, monthly quota on Studio.

All non-AI features keep working normally — worldbuilding, maps, schemas, exports, the quest editor. For AI features, you can optionally enable OpenAI as a backup provider in your Mimir settings. When enabled, if Anthropic is unavailable, Mimir automatically routes to OpenAI temporarily. You'll see a banner when this happens. If you'd rather wait for Claude to return, just leave failover disabled — AI features will pause gracefully and resume when the service recovers.

AI features (Ask Mimir, Translator) require a connection since they route through our servers. If you lose connectivity mid-session, everything except AI continues working normally.
Your world files live on your computer — we never see them unless you use an AI feature. When you use Ask Mimir or the Translator, the relevant context is sent to our AI broker (currently Anthropic's Claude) to process your request. We do not store, train on, or retain your world data after the request completes. The AI provider's standard data handling policies apply to the brief transit period. If you need stricter guarantees for unreleased IP, contact us to discuss options.
